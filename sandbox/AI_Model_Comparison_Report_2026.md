# AI Model Comparison Report 2026

This report provides a comparative analysis of Kimi 2.5, GLM 4.7, and the latest Claude 4.5 models (Opus, Sonnet, Haiku) as of 2026, focusing on their features, performance, and use cases.

## 1. Kimi 2.5

Kimi 2.5 is an open-source visual agentic intelligence model. It has gained attention for its capabilities in handling complex tasks and its potential for open-source development. Key features and performance highlights include:

*   **Open-Source Nature:** Promotes community collaboration and customization [1, 2].
*   **Visual Agentic Intelligence:** Designed for tasks requiring visual understanding and multi-step agentic workflows [1].
*   **Complex Task Handling:** Capable of addressing intricate problems and scenarios [1].
*   **Coding Agent:** Demonstrates strong capabilities as a coding agent, assisting in software development [5].

**Use Cases:** Open-source AI development, visual task automation, complex problem-solving, and coding assistance [1, 5].

## 2. GLM 4.7

GLM 4.7, developed by Zhipu AI, is positioned as a powerful reasoning powerhouse, particularly with its Flash variant. It's designed for real-world development environments and aims to be a strong contender in the AI landscape. Key features and performance highlights include:

*   **Reasoning Powerhouse:** Emphasizes strong reasoning capabilities for complex logical tasks [7, 8].
*   **Real-World Development Environments:** Tailored for practical application in various development scenarios [9].
*   **Local AI Coding Assistant (Flash):** The Flash variant is highlighted as an ultimate guide to local AI coding assistance [8].
*   **Efficiency:** Designed for high performance and efficient operation [7].
*   **Competitive with OpenAI:** Positions itself as a competitor to OpenAI's models, especially in the Chinese market [9].

**Use Cases:** Complex reasoning tasks, coding assistance, real-world application development, and enterprise solutions [7, 8, 9].

## 3. Claude 4.5 Series (Opus, Sonnet, Haiku)

Anthropic's Claude 4.5 series, released in late 2025 and early 2026, includes three distinct models: Opus, Sonnet, and Haiku, each optimized for different use cases, offering a spectrum of intelligence, speed, and cost-efficiency [10, 11, 12].

### 3.1. Claude Opus 4.5

Opus 4.5 is the most intelligent model in the series, excelling in complex reasoning, advanced coding, and detailed creative work. It shows significant advancements in software engineering benchmarks and agentic robustness [10, 11, 13].

*   **Coding Performance:** Achieved 80.9% on SWE-bench Verified, surpassing human engineering candidates and competitors like GPT-5.1-Codex-Max (77.9%) and Gemini 3 Pro (76.2%) [11, 13].
*   **Abstract Reasoning:** Demonstrated a significant leap in abstract reasoning, scoring 37.6% on ARC-AGI-2 [11].
*   **Agentic Capabilities:** Excels at long-horizon, autonomous tasks, with improved planning and multi-step execution [10]. It also shows strong resistance to prompt injection attacks [11].
*   **Context Window:** Features a 200K context window, enabling it to handle long-context storytelling and complex projects [10, 13].
*   **Cost-Efficiency:** Priced at $5 per million input tokens and $25 per million output tokens, a 66% reduction from previous Opus models [13].

**Use Cases:** Complex reasoning, advanced coding, deep research, multi-system bug fixes, code migration, refactoring, and enterprise workflows [10, 11, 13].

### 3.2. Claude Sonnet 4.5

Sonnet 4.5 offers a balance of intelligence and efficiency, making it suitable for most AI applications. It is particularly strong in agentic coding and computer use [12, 14].

*   **Coding Performance:** Excellent for agentic coding, capable of tasks across the entire software development life cycle, from planning to bug fixes [12, 14].
*   **Computer Use:** Excels in reliably handling browser-based tasks, showing a significant leap in OSWorld benchmark scores (61.4% vs. 42.2% for Sonnet 4) [14].
*   **Long-Running Tasks:** Can maintain focus for over 30 hours on complex, multi-step tasks [14].
*   **Domain-Specific Knowledge:** Improved performance in domains like finance, law, medicine, and STEM [14].
*   **Pricing:** Priced at $3 per million input tokens and $15 per million output tokens [12, 14].

**Use Cases:** Customer-facing agents, production coding workflows, content generation at scale, real-time research tasks, financial analysis, cybersecurity, and business tasks [12, 14].

### 3.3. Claude Haiku 4.5

Haiku 4.5 is the fastest and most cost-efficient model in the Claude 4.5 series, delivering near-frontier intelligence at a fraction of the cost and latency [15, 16, 17].

*   **Speed and Cost-Efficiency:** Runs 4-5 times faster than Sonnet 4.5 at a fraction of the cost, with pricing at $1 per million input tokens and $5 per million output tokens [15, 17].
*   **Coding Performance:** Achieves similar levels of coding performance to Sonnet 4, and 90% of Sonnet 4.5's performance in agentic coding evaluations [15, 17].
*   **Computer Use:** Even surpasses Sonnet 4 in certain computer use tasks [15].
*   **Context Window:** Supports a 200,000-token context window with advanced features like extended thinking, computer use, and context awareness [16].

**Use Cases:** Real-time, low-latency tasks like chat assistants, customer service agents, pair programming, multi-agent orchestration, and cost-efficient AI solutions [15, 17].

In 2026, the AI model landscape is rich with diverse offerings catering to various needs. Kimi 2.5 and GLM 4.7 represent strong contenders in the open-source and specialized reasoning domains, respectively. The Claude 4.5 series from Anthropic provides a comprehensive suite of models, allowing users to choose based on their specific requirements for intelligence, speed, and cost.

Claude Opus 4.5 stands out for its cutting-edge performance in coding and complex reasoning, making it ideal for demanding enterprise applications. Claude Sonnet 4.5 offers a versatile option with strong agentic and computer-use capabilities at a balanced price point.
